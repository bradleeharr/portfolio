Passive Radar Signal Processing Project

This project uses passive radar techniques to detect and track aerial targets. Instead of relying on conventional radar systems, commercial FM radio stations are leveraged as illuminators of opportunity.

Detection

The detection process involved processing the signals received from the FM radio stations. By analyzing these signals, the system could detect the presence of aerial targets within range.

Tracking

Once a target was detected, the next step was tracking. Through advanced signal processing techniques, the system was capable of monitoring the movement and trajectory of the detected targets.

Results

The project successfully demonstrated that passive radar systems can effectively detect and track aerial targets using signals from commercial FM radio stations, paving the way for more cost-effective and stealthy radar systems.
