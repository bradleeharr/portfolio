Stereo Image Quality Assessment Project

This project delves into the complex issue of blind quality assessment of stereo images. By leveraging the tools of Fourier analysis, the Gabor transform, and luminance and contrast metrics, we developed innovative techniques to evaluate the quality of stereo images.

Fourier Analysis

Fourier analysis was the first method we utilized for assessing the quality of stereo images. It involved analyzing the frequency components of the images and comparing the differences to determine quality discrepancies.

Gabor Transform

Next, we employed the Gabor transform, a technique used to analyze the spatial frequency content in localized regions of the stereo images. This gave us further insights into localized discrepancies and details that might not be noticeable at a global level.

Luminance and Contrast Metrics

Lastly, we used luminance and contrast metrics to measure the perceived brightness and contrast in different parts of the stereo images. This allowed us to account for perceptual aspects of visual quality assessment, completing a comprehensive evaluation of the stereo image quality.
